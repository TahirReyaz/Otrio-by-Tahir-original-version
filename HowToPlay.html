O-TRIO

  • If you too dwell in this world, you may have come across O-TRIO's ancestor 'Tic-Tac-Toe'.
  • Check out the difference in this YouTube video: OTRIO not Tic-Tac-Toe
  • Watch this YouTube video: How to play Otrio or keep reading this page.
  • At least 2 and atmost 4 players can play this game at a time.
  • Inintailly the board looks like this:
  • In 4 player mode: Each player chooses one colour and drags their pieces into any spaces when their turn comes.
  • Drag the pieces on the central part of the board.
  • There are 3 ways to win:
  • Either place three peices of same colour in same space.
  • Or same kind and colour of piece in a row, column or diagonal, just like Tic Tak Tic-Tac-Toe.
  • Or consecutive increasing or decreasing size pieces in a row, column or diagonal.
  • Different colored pieces can be placed in the same space and the conditions to win will still hold true.
